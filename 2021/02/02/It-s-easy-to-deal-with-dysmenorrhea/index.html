It's easy to deal with dysmenorrhea

When I was a child, I loved chasing after and fighting with boys, which made me believe that men and women had the same fighting capacity. Until one day, I began to menstruate, and the severe pain made my fighting capacity smaller and smaller until I became negative. Since then, regular or irregular dysmenorrhea enables me to see the gentle smile of God in the dizziness of pain. While the consciousness of the brain is fuzzy, the pain in the lower abdomen brings the psychedelic mind back to reality.

The pain also makes me think that menstruation, which makes woman be afraid of both its coming and not coming, is just a bug left by God on women. Most of a woman’s long life is spent playing games with it.

Actually, dysmenorrhea is very common among women. 50-90% of women suffer from dysmenorrhea. Severe dysmenorrhea is like robbery, which makes people lose the ability to move and seriously affects their daily work, study and life.

According to statistics of relevant research, 80% of primary dysmenorrhea can be effectively relieved by painkillers. However, due to the stigmatization of painkillers, many women prefer to try various folk prescriptions rather than take painkillers. Many girls can only endure severe pain because they are afraid of the side effects of painkillers.

This includes me in the past. Until I was in Canada, I went to the pharmacy because of my menstrual pain. The pharmacist explained the principle to me and told me that some painkillers were completely acceptable. After referring to a lot of literature and taking relevant painkillers, I can’t help sighing that painkillers are the light of women!

Correct the name for painkillers

A lot of people may be faced with such confusions: some say that painkillers are addictive, some say that they are not addictive. In fact, they may confuse these two kinds of painkillers (narcotic and non narcotic).

  1. The painkillers with the risk of addiction is opium drugs, which can’t be bought at will and they are prescription drugs and strictly controlled.
  2. The non steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs eaten during dysmenorrhea has nno risk of addiction.

For example, ibuprofen relives the pain through the inhibition of prostaglandin secretion. Compared with opium painkillers, NSAIDs are more like soldiers fighting outside, helping the brain calm down external chaos. They work hard at the front line, and will not make the brain get addicted to it.

The side effects of taking a few painkillers occasionally are really insignificant compared with severe dysmenorrhea. Moreover, ibuprofen can be hardly detected after 2 days in vivo, and there is no need to worry about the accumulation of toxicity.

Taking me as an example, I recommend girls suffering from dysmenorrhea to try painkillers bravely, in which Ibuprofen is the priority, because it has both excellent effect and safety. Dysmenorrhea can also be relieved by non drug means, such as abdominal hot compress, non menstrual aerobic exercise, etc. Finally, I wish women all over the world to stay away from dysmenorrhea!
