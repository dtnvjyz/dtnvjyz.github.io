Protective the eye to prevent myopia is of vital importance

I still remember that only 4 of the more than 50 classmates in the high school class did not wear glasses, and now almost everyone around me has myopia. In retrospect, when I was about at the sixth grade of elementary school. I firmly believed that it looked cool to wear glasses, therefore, I deliberately developed a bad habit of using my eyes in an unhealthy way. When I had a slight tendency towards myopia, my parents had already taken me to see the doctor, hoping to control pseudomyopia. Now I really regret it! Once myopia occurs, it is irreversible. Compared with correction and treatment, prevention is the key.

According to a survey on the rising rates of myopia and high myopia around the world published by on scientific journal Ophthalmology of the American Academy of Ophthalmology, by 2050, 4.76 billion people will have myopia (which accounts for 49.8% of the world’s population); 0.938 billion people suffer from high myopia (which accounta for 9.8% of the world’s population). In other words, by 2050, more than 50% of the world’s population will have myopia. The prevention and control of myopia is already an issue that should be paid attention to by the whole world.

Everyone may have the gene of myopia

There are many rumors about the inheritance of myopia: some say that it will be inherited, some say that it will not be inherited; some say that it is inherited only after 600 degrees… In fact, they are not all true. Myopia is indeed related to inheritance, but what a child inherits is a constitution that is prone to myopia, instead of myopia itself.

Therefore, prevention of myopia should start from the parents. If both parents have myopia,they should indeed keep alert, but acquired factors should also be taken into consideration.

“Staying in the house” makes more and more people to develop myopia

Seeing objects in a close distance plays an important role in developing myopia, but it is not the only factor. It is generally believed that reading a book or playing mobile phone keeps our eyes focused on a close range for a long time, making us lose sight over time, which is probably the most deeply rooted cause of myopia.

Indeed, a large number of studies have found that there is a significant correlation between using eyes in a short distance and myopia, and the prevalence of myopia is often higher in areas with higher universal education.

Less outdoor activities have a significant correlation with the occurrence of myopia

At present, more and more studies have confirmed that the increase in outdoor activities under strong light in childhood can significantly reduce the risk of myopia and delay the development of myopia to a certain extent.

Outdoor activities cover a wide range, including walking outdoors, playing, having a picnic, and even reading, etc.
Australian scholars have conducted a three-year follow-up study on more than 4,000 primary and secondary school students, and reached the conclusion: You can effectively prevent myopia as long as you spend enough time outdoors, whether you are exercising or not. It is believed by the academic community that this may be because outdoor light has an impact on the development of the eye’s refractive system.

Don’t think that myopia can be solved by simply wearing an extra pair of glasses.

Generally speaking, the higher the degree of myopia, the higher the risk of blinding diseases such as retinal detachment, and the worse the quality of life in the future, it is imperative to prevent and control myopia.

For children, as long as they use their eyes scientifically, carry out more outdoor activities, and have a reasonable diet and ensure adequate sleep, myopia can still be prevented or controlled to a large extent.
