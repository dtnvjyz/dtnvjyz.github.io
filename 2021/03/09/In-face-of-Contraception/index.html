In face of Contraception

Every adult, sooner or later, will inevitably come into contact with sex, and we are destined to deal with contraception. Before having a clear plan to have a baby, please spare no efforts to take contraception seriously.

Perhaps because I have heard many examples living in a doctor’s family, I believe teenagers are lack of sex education. Biology courses teach where babies come from, but contraception is always a topic that people don’t want to mention. Even some married adults don’t know enough recognition of correct contraception.

Today, let’s talk about common contraception methods. Are they really useful? Which is better?

Unreliable contraception methods

Due to the lack of sex education, human beings have various “unconventional methods” based on physiological laws or word of mouth. However, the actual effect is bad. These seemingly reasonable contraceptive methods are actually a kind of gambling.

  1. Unprotected sex during lactation
    Ovulation can’t be prevented for 100% during lactation. Some women judge whether to ovulate by whether they resume menstruation after giving birth. In fact, most woman ovulate before they resume menstruation.
  2. Contraception in safe period
    Sperm can survive in a woman’s body for up to 5 days, and a woman’s ovulation period is also affected by various factors. Therefore, it is quite unreliable to carry out contraception in safe period.
  3. In vitro ejaculation
    Before ejaculation, the fluid secreted by men contains sperm, in addition, this may lead to functional non-ejaculation syndrome.

Reliable methods of contraception

1.Take medicine

Regulate estrogen and progesterone, make the body believe that it has become pregnant, thus suspend ovulation. Common medicine includes: contraceptive pills, contraceptive implants under the skin(99.95% success rate).

Short-term contraceptives (99.7% success rate) VS emergency contraceptives (80% success rate)

The application situation is different:

short-term contraceptives can be used for a long time, usually the oral medicine-taking time is 21 days of each menstrual cycle, suitable for ordinary women in childbearing age.

Emergency contraceptives cannot be taken frequently. They are usually taken orally after sex life, with the time being within 72 hours. It is suitable for women who have suffered accidental injuries or have sex without protection.

The mechanism of action is different:

the progesterone in short-term contraceptives is very close to human progesterone, which can not only terminate pregnancy, but also help prevent female diseases, such as ovarian cancer, endometrial cancer, etc. and it is of certain effect on regulating female hormone levels.

However, the emergency contraceptive pill is only a remedy after having sex, which will change the women’s menstrual cycle, leading to female endocrine disorders, therefore, it has high risk of failure, and obvious side effects, and can only be used for emergency.

2.Tools

Prevent the sperm from meeting the egg, or prevent the fertilized egg from implanting and developing. Common tools include: condoms(98% success rate), intrauterine devices(99.8% success rate).

3.Surgical methods

Use surgery to close the vas deferens/fallopian tube to prevent the release of sperm or eggs. Common surgeries include: Fallopian tube(99% success rate) / vas deferens ligation surgery(99.9% success rate).

Of course, no matter which contraceptive method you choose, it must be based on actual physical conditions of both partners. There is no best, only the most suitable. Finally, I want to remind everyone that, except for condoms, no method of contraception can prevent sexually transmitted diseases.

***Data of “success rate” mentioned in the paper originates from UTD **
